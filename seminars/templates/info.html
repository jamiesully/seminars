{{meta_description}} On this site, seminar means a talk series (which might be a conference), and a talk is a one-time event that occurs as part of a seminar.

Browse

The front page lists upcoming talks. You can filter them by topic to tailor them to your interests.

Search

You can search for seminars and talks satisfying various criteria, including specifying keywords within talk abstracts.

Subscribe

After registering, you can export selected seminars and talks to your own calendar (e.g., Google calendar, Outlook or iCal). When you subscribe to a seminar, new talks will be pushed to your calendar automatically.

Attend

Many seminars include livestreaming links for direct access. Some organizers make these links visible only to users logged in to a mathseminars.org account.

Create

If you are organizing a seminar, we aim to make it easy to add your seminar to the site. Once you create a new seminar (requires an endorsed account), you can add upcoming or historical talks.

Organize

Seminar organizers can add upcoming talks easily: defaults are filled in from the seminar but are editable on a per-talk basis. Sharing a talk's edit link with speakers allows them to update their own title and abstract. Seminar information can be exported to formats suitable for your own website or department calendar.

Questions? Error reports? Suggestions?

If your issue is not addressed by the FAQ, please contact us!
